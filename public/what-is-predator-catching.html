What is Predator Catching?

Overview

Predator catching involves individuals or groups posing as minors on social media, chat rooms, or online forums to engage potential sexual predators. The goal is to gather evidence of intent to exploit or harm a minor sexually.

Methods

  • Undercover Interaction: Creating fake profiles posing as minors.
  • Gathering Evidence: Documenting conversations, including explicit messages or proposals for meeting.
  • Confrontation: Arranging a meeting and confronting the individual with their actions.
  • Legal Involvement: Turning over evidence to law enforcement for possible legal action.

Legality

Laws and regulations regarding predator catching vary by jurisdiction and prosecutor preference. For example, in Ohio, evidence produced by non-law enforcement is often thrown out of court. You can read more about this in the following articles:

  • Vigilantes posing as kids to out predators deemed unlawful by police (USA Today)
  • How regular citizens are catching would-be predators (Local 12) - Ausdenmoore says Ohio law is clear: The victim has to be a real minor or a police officer posing as one

Community Impact

Predator catching often garners significant public attention and community reaction. It can lead to awareness of online risks but also fuel public shaming or vigilantism.
